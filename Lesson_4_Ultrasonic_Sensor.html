🔊 How Ultrasonic Sensors Work

🎵 Sound Wave Principle

Ultrasonic sensors use high-frequency sound waves (above human hearing) to measure distance through echo location, similar to how bats navigate in the dark.

📡 Ultrasonic Wave Process

📤

TRANSMIT

Send sound wave

🎯

REFLECT

Bounce off object

📥

RECEIVE

Detect echo

Sound travels at 343 meters/second in air

⚡ Challenge: Detect the Treasure

Starting from shore, navigate across the bay and use sonar to detect hidden sea treasures (blocks). Once detected, stop and bring the treasure back to the port.

🚧 Mission Specification

Challenge: Starting from the Green Square or Single Straight black line, the robot will move forward and detect an object in one of the six squares. Once the robot finds the object, turn and bring it back to the starting position.

🎯 Mission Parameters

  • Starting Position: Green square or one end of the black straight line
  • Target Zone: Back to the starting position
  • Success Criteria: Detect the object with the ultrasonic sensor and use the robot arm to bring it back.

💡 Navigation Strategy

Using the information about the sensors on the top of the Spike app, find out the value that the ultrasonic sensor gives when the block is near the robot. That value means something is near the robot! For best results, test this on the mat with both the robot and block positioned correctly.

🤖 Programming Approach

🌊 Movement Sequence Planning

Step 1: Move robot forward until an object is detected by the ultrasonic sensor.
Step 2: Have the robot turn towards the object and use the arm to grab the block.
Step 3: Turn back and move backwards to starting spot. Or turn towards starting spot and move forward.
Step 4: Adjust path as needed.
Student's Example Command:

"Reset my robot's relative degrees moved. Move my robot forward until the ultrasonic sensor detects an object at the distance of 200 or under. Record the relative degrees moved up till this point and store it in a variable called "distance." Then, turn 90 degrees to the right, raise the arm by 45 degrees clockwise, move forward 1 inch, lower the arm by 45 degrees, and move backward 1 inch. At last, turn the robot left 90 degrees and move backward by the value stored in the distance variable."
